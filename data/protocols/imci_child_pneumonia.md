# IMCI Child Pneumonia and Cough Protocol Notes

Source: Demonstration protocol packet inspired by public IMCI-style guidance. Replace with official Ministry-approved documents before real deployment.

For children age 2 to 59 months with cough or difficulty breathing, assess general danger signs first: unable to drink or breastfeed, vomiting everything, convulsions, lethargy or unconsciousness, chest indrawing, stridor, or severe respiratory distress.

Fast breathing thresholds used in many IMCI-style protocols: age 2 to 11 months: respiratory rate 50 breaths/minute or more. Age 12 to 59 months: respiratory rate 40 breaths/minute or more.

If fast breathing is present without severe danger signs, classify as suspected pneumonia or same-day condition according to the local protocol. Give first dose treatment only where authorized by local protocol and refer or follow up according to classification.

If chest indrawing, severe respiratory distress, bluish lips, inability to drink, convulsion, or lethargy is present, treat as emergency/danger sign and arrange urgent referral.
