# Fever General Assessment Protocol Notes

Source: Demonstration fever assessment packet. Replace with official national fever, malaria, and infection protocols before real use.

For fever, assess age, measured temperature, duration, malaria risk area, cough or breathing difficulty, neck stiffness, convulsions, lethargy, hydration, feeding, rash, and caregiver ability to return.

Young infants with fever are high risk and should receive urgent assessment/referral according to the local protocol.

Fever with danger signs such as convulsion, lethargy, unconsciousness, inability to drink, severe dehydration, or respiratory distress requires urgent referral.

Fever without danger signs still requires same-day assessment, counseling, and follow-up instructions based on the local protocol.
