# Maternal and Pregnancy Danger Sign Protocol Notes

Source: Demonstration maternal danger-sign packet. Replace with official Ministry maternal health protocols before real deployment.

Pregnancy danger signs include vaginal bleeding, severe headache, blurred vision, swelling of face or hands, convulsions, severe abdominal pain, fever, foul-smelling discharge, difficulty breathing, and reduced fetal movement.

Bleeding during pregnancy or postpartum is an emergency warning sign. The HEW should arrange urgent referral, notify the supervisor or referral facility according to local protocol, and avoid delays.

For pregnant or postpartum clients, ask gestational age, bleeding amount, dizziness/fainting, abdominal pain, blood pressure where available, fever, and transport availability.
